## **0.** Preamble

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the G2Project-main project by Dayo Akanji ("We" or "Us").

Software is deeply embedded in all aspects of our lives and it is important that it empowers, rather than restrict us. Free Software gives rights to use, understand, adapt and share software to everybody. These rights help support other fundamental freedoms like freedom of speech, press and privacy.

The development of Free Software can follow many patterns. In some cases, development is handled by a single programmer or a small group of people. Usually however, the creation and maintenance of software is a complex process that requires the contribution of many individuals. This also affects ownership of the rights to the software. In the latter case, rights in software are owned jointly by a large number of individuals and the resulting fragmentation of ownership rights can impose restrictions in the use, understanding, adaptation and sharing of software.

To tackle this issue, some projects require a full copyright assignment to be signed by all contributors. The problem with such assignments however, is that they often lack checks and balances that would protect the contributors from potential abuse of power from the new copyright holder.

The Fiduciary License Agreement was created by the Free Software Foundation Europe e.V. to concentrate all deciding power within one entity to avoid the fragmentation of rights on one hand, while preventing that single entity from abusing its power on the other. It does this by ensuring that software covered under the Fiduciary License Agreement will forever remain Free Software.

This process only serves for the transfer of economic rights. So-called moral rights (e.g. authors rights to be identified as authors) remain with the original authors and are inalienable.
